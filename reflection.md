- Which methods did you decide to `overload`, and why?
For cafe, I chose to overload the "sellCoffee" method, because you do not always need sugar and cream in it. When people only say can I have a coffee, then it can just be black coffee. I also chose to overload the constructor so that people do not have to enter number of floors and address, because I will assume my map's address is Smith College, and also if you can only access one floor, what is the point of having a building with more floors than that?

For library, I overloaded the constructor for Library for the same reason. If the library does not have an address and floors entered, we will use Smith College as the default address, and 3 floors as the number of floors.

For house, I chose to overload the constructor of House, since most of Smith's houses do not have a dining room, and the name is enough, we might not need the address to know where the house is (we can just say it is at Smith College). I also overload the moveIn method, since I feel like it would be fun if we can move 2 students in at a time (this is not uncommon, there might be twin moving in!). I made this simple so that both students must not already be residents in order for both to move in.
